About the Map

This world map presents data on air pollution indices as calculated by the Institute for Health Metrics and Evaluation (IHME). IHME has developed its own metrics for assessing air quality, measuring air pollutant particles in parts per billion. Figures 1.1, 1.2, 1.3, and 1.4 each provide different perspectives on IHME's air quality index.

In summary, a higher index value indicates an increased risk of illness due to water contamination, compromised food supplies, and the spread of diseases.

How to Read the Map

To find out the specific air pollution levels in various locations, simply hover over the countries on the map that interest you.

About the Chart

The chart shows the trend in Air Pollution deaths accoding to the 2019 IHME index for 1990, 2000, 2010 and 2016. Select a continent from the Continent Selection to understand the trends

Asia has the highest average share of deaths due to air pollution, followed by Oceania and Africa. Europe has the lowest average death rate (IHME 2019) at 2016 .

What does this mean?

The data suggests that the share of deaths attributable to air pollution is decreasing globally over time, according to the IHME index. This could imply a number of things:

Improved Air Quality, Improved healthcare systems, medical treatments, and overall a Shift in Causes of Death.

North Korea and Solomon Islands remain top 5 worst in both 1990 and 2016

North Korea maintained its position as the most polluted country, however, its air pollution level declined from 17.19 to 16.51 in 36 years. Similarly, Solomon Islands experienced a decrease in pollution levels and moved up in ranking from 5th to 4th place.

On the other hand, new countries emerged in the 2016 data, notably Bangladesh, India, and Nepal. These countries were not present in the 1990 dataset. Conversely, countries like China, Cambodia, and Tajikistan, which were part of the 1990 list, are missing from the 2016 data.

Overall, while some countries have managed to reduce their air pollution levels, new countries have appeared on the list, indicating shifts in the global landscape of air pollution.
